The Goal

Your task is to implement a scoring calculator for a game called Entanglement. Based on the defined rules, determine how many points the player should receive for the current move after placing their tile on the board, as well as the exact position where the current path ends.

  Rules

The game is played on a board where the player gradually places hexagonal tiles. Each tile contains 12 entrances numbered 0â€“ 11, forming 6 distinct paths. Each entrance is used exactly once:

Example of a single tile with numbered entrances.

Game rules:

  • The player always starts the game at coordinate [0, -1] through entrance 0.
  • The player always places the next tile on the cell where their current position is located (so the first tile is placed at [0, -1]).
  • After placing a tile, the player follows the newly created path.
  • The player continues along this path until it ends on an empty cell, outside the board, or back at the base located at [0, 0].
  • The game ends when the path leaves the board or returns to the base at [0, 0].

Scoring:
A single move consists of placing one tile on the board. After placing it, the player automatically follows the created path. For each segment of the path traversed during this move, the player earns 1, 2, 3, 4, ... points respectively. For example, if the path goes through 5 segments, the player earns 15 points.

Required output:
For every move requested by the game, output earned_points, x, y, entrance, where x and y are the coordinates of the destination cell (according to the coordinate system below), and entrance is the entry point on that cell from which the player will start the next path.
